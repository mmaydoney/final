Climbing with Sean

I haven't been on a climbing route in over 25 years. At 61 years old I wonder if I have a few ascents left in me and if I could do them with my son. One of the routes I have in mind is the Regular Route on Fairview Dome in Yosemite park just east of Tenaya Lake. This is a really fun 5-6 pitch climb primarily a 5.7 to 5.8 difficulty level with a couple of 5.9+ moves one of which is a really fun overhang. The walk off down the back of Fairview Dome is easy and has great views. I've led this route aproimately 6-8 times and know how cool it is.

2017 Climbing Goal!

The route I'm most interested in checking out with Sean is the East Face Route of Mt. Whitney. There is an incredible exposed fresh air traverse that makes you feel like your in air. This climb has a long approach and you need to aclimate to the altitude. It ranges from scrambling up 4.0 to 5.7 difficulty and ends at the summit.

The first step is getting in climbing shape which means a lot of yoga, biking and climbing. I still have my old harness and I need to do some work to fit into it again. A year of preparation will be what makes the East Face Route part of this dream happen.
